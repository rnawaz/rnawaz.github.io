About Me

Building Bridges Between
AI Research & Real-World Impact

I am a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in the Department of Computer Science and Electronic Engineering at the University of Essex, and a Technical Trainer and Consultant in Data & AI with experience designing and delivering high-impact learning programmes across machine learning, Python, and applied AI. My research sits at the intersection of Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI), neural signal processing, and applied machine learning, with a growing focus on health informatics and digital health, investigating how intelligent systems can decode neural and physiological signals to enable assistive technologies, remote patient monitoring, and data-driven clinical decision support.

I have served as programme lead and designed a UK Level 6 Data Science Apprenticeship Programme, embedding generative AI, responsible AI, and enterprise-ready deployment practices. My teaching philosophy is firmly rooted in hands-on, scenario-based learning that connects AI concepts to real business outcomes.

Endorsed by the Royal Society of Engineering as an Exceptional Research Promise under the Global Talent scheme, I have worked at leading institutions in the UK, Austria, and Malaysia, and have collaborated with the NHS and industry partners on real-world AI projects.
